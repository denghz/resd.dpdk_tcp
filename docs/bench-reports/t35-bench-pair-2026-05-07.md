## Notes

**C=64 small-W dip**: C=64,W=256 (0.434 Gbps) and C=64,W=64 (0.279 Gbps)
are below the C=16 row. At small write sizes, 64 connections exhaust the
32768-mbuf pool during warmup; send_bytes returns Ok(0) for most calls
during the measurement window. The fix prevents this from cascading into
subsequent buckets.

**Pool wobble**: C=64,W=256 post-close shows 32767 (1 mbuf short); the
following C=1,W=1024 pre-open also shows 32767 then recovers to 32768
after that bucket's close. A single in-flight mbuf at the snapshot moment
— not a leak (previous T33/T34 runs were clean to 32768).

**Peak**: ~2.779 Gbps at C=4,W=4096 and 2.770 Gbps at C=16,W=4096 —
highest recorded across T33/T34/T35 runs. Natural EC2 network variability;
no code changed between T34 and T35.

**Third consecutive clean run**: T33 (16:24 UTC), T34 (17:11 UTC), T35
(17:55 UTC) all produce 28/28 non-zero buckets with pool recovering to
32768. The snd_retrans leak fix is stable.
