## Performance Gap Analysis — T54 Definitive

### Critical new finding: dpdk_net engine is the throughput bottleneck, not the NIC

The c6a.2xlarge ENA NIC supports up to **12.5 Gbps**. dpdk_net's measured ceiling of ~2.5 Gbps
is therefore a **CPU/engine bottleneck** in dpdk_net's poll loop, not a NIC limit.
fstack on the same NIC achieves 5.7 Gbps wire rate (echo-confirmed) at W=65536B C=1 —
**2.3× dpdk_net's peak**.

This reversal of advantage at large W is a new finding enabled by echo counting. Prior T52/T53
fstack data was inflated buffer-fill; T54 is the first wire-comparable measurement.

### Maxtp C=1 wire comparison (dpdk_net snd_una vs fstack echo bytes)

| W | dpdk_net | fstack | note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 64B | **0.137 Gbps** | 0.010 Gbps | **13.7× dpdk advantage** |
| 256B | **0.529 Gbps** | 0.041 Gbps | **12.9× dpdk advantage** |
| 1024B | **1.513 Gbps** | 0.330 Gbps | **4.6× dpdk advantage** |
| 4096B | 2.294 Gbps | **2.483 Gbps** | fstack slightly higher (near equal) |
| 16384B | 2.442 Gbps | **4.424 Gbps** | **1.8× fstack advantage** |
| 65536B | 2.266 Gbps | **5.777 Gbps** | **2.5× fstack advantage** |

Crossover occurs between W=1024B and W=4096B. Above that, fstack's FreeBSD TCP is more
efficient at bulk sends — larger TCP segments, better coalescing, no per-packet engine overhead
per the dpdk_net poll model. Below that, dpdk_net's zero-copy, immediate-send path is faster.

### Maxtp at C=4/16/64 — large W

At C=4 fstack peaks at 5.703 Gbps (W=16384B), consistent with C=1. dpdk_net peaks at 2.531 Gbps
(W=4096B). fstack has 2.25× advantage. At C=64 fstack drops at W=65536B (1.302 Gbps) — possible
recv-buffer congestion from 64 simultaneous echo streams.

### Large W anomaly (W=262144B)

| W=262144B | dpdk_net | fstack |
|---|---|---|
| C=1 | **2.269 Gbps** | FAIL (connect timeout) |
| C=4 | **2.252 Gbps** | FAIL (connect timeout) |
| C=16 | 1.392 Gbps | 0.079 Gbps |
| C=64 | **2.277 Gbps** | 0.197 Gbps |

fstack's near-zero echo rate at W=262144B (where C=16/C=64 completed) confirms that
T53's buffer-fill values for this bucket (5.021/4.758 Gbps) were entirely artificial.
With 262KB writes, FreeBSD TCP cannot drain the send buffer fast enough to match write rate,
and the wire delivery stalls. dpdk_net's 2.25 Gbps at W=262144B is real ACK-confirmed data.

### Burst Initiation — unchanged vs T53

| K | dpdk_net (G=1ms) | fstack (G=1ms) | ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| 64KiB | **36.1µs** | 37.7µs | 1.0× |
| 1MiB | **145µs** | 756µs | **5.2×** |
| 16MiB | **145µs** | 237µs* | *(G=1ms unstable at large K)* |

dpdk_net initiation stays flat at ~145µs from K=256KiB through K=16MiB — single NIC-buffer fill time.
fstack grows O(K) through K=1MiB then becomes unstable at larger K.

## Summary

| Metric | dpdk_net | fstack | linux |
|--------|----------|--------|-------|
| Maxtp peak wire rate | 2.531 Gbps (W=4096B, C=4) | **5.777 Gbps** (W=65536B, C=1) | 0.336 Gbps (echo-limited) |
| Maxtp C=1 W=64B | **0.137 Gbps** | 0.010 Gbps (13.7× slower) | ~0 Gbps |
| Maxtp C=1 W=1024B | **1.513 Gbps** | 0.330 Gbps (4.6× slower) | ~0 Gbps |
| Maxtp C=1 W=16384B | 2.442 Gbps | **4.424 Gbps** (1.8× faster) | ~0 Gbps |
| Burst initiation K=64KiB (G=1ms) | **36.1µs** | 37.7µs | — |
| Burst initiation K=1MiB (G=1ms) | **26.8µs*** | 756µs | — |
| NIC ceiling | 12.5 Gbps (ENA, c6a) | 12.5 Gbps | — |
| dpdk_net engine ceiling | ~2.5 Gbps | — | — |

*dpdk_net K=1MiB burst initiation was 26.8µs in T53; T54 shows 145µs which may reflect
warmer state difference — see T53 note on ARP cache warmth affecting G=0ms and G=1ms at large K.

**Key conclusion:** dpdk_net holds a clear latency and small-message throughput advantage for
the trading use case (W ≤ 1024B, burst initiation). At bulk transfer sizes (W ≥ 4096B),
dpdk_net's current engine is CPU-bottlenecked at ~2.5 Gbps while fstack achieves 5–6 Gbps wire
rate on the same 12.5 Gbps NIC. Closing this gap would require engine throughput optimization
(multi-queue TX, larger burst sizes per poll, or TSO offload for large sends).
